What is PBIS?

Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) is a proactive approach to improving school climate and student behavior. It focuses on teaching expected behaviors and rewarding positive actions. Our school uses PBIS to create a safe, respectful, and supportive learning environment for all students. Through Tiered supports, every child receives the interventions they need to succeed. Together, we foster a culture of respect, responsibility, and kindness.

PBIS Newsletter

Understanding PBIS: PBIS helps build positive behaviors by teaching clear expectations and rewarding success. It supports parents by offering tips to reinforce positive behavior at home.

Parent Support Tips:

  • Set clear expectations at home.
  • Use consistent rewards for good behavior.
  • Communicate regularly with teachers.

Key PBIS Terms: Behavior Matrix, Tier 1, Reinforcement, Data Collection, Infractions

PBIS Tiers Overview

Tier 1 - Universal Supports

All students receive proactive teaching of behavioral expectations and schoolwide positive reinforcement.

Tier 2 - Targeted Supports

Students requiring additional support receive small group interventions and progress monitoring.

Tier 3 - Intensive Supports

Individualized interventions are provided for students with significant behavioral challenges, including functional behavior assessments.

Staff Buy-in Strategies

  • Collaborative planning and decision-making.
  • Regular professional development on PBIS.
  • Recognition and rewards for staff implementation efforts.

Data Collection Sheets

We use multiple data collection forms to monitor behavior across all tiers:

  • Tier 1 Behavior Tracking Form - monitors general schoolwide behavior.
  • Tier 2 Intervention Progress Form - tracks small group support progress.
  • Tier 3 Individual Behavior Plan Data Sheet - records intensive intervention data.
  • Office Referral Form - documents major infractions and disciplinary actions.

Data is collected weekly by staff and reviewed monthly by PBIS team for decision-making.

Behavior Matrix

School Setting Expected Behaviors
Classroom Listen attentively, raise hand, respect peers, follow directions
Hallways Walk quietly, keep hands to self, stay to the right
Playground Share equipment, use kind words, include others
Cafeteria Use inside voice, clean up, wait patiently in line

Tier 1 Reward System

Our reward system encourages positive behavior at all levels:

  • Daily: Verbal praise and “Caught Being Good” tickets issued by all staff.
  • Weekly: Ticket drawings for small prizes during assemblies.
  • Monthly: Recognition certificates sent home; parent involvement encouraged.
  • Annual: PBIS Celebration event with trophies and community rewards.

Major and Minor Infractions

Infraction Type Description Examples
Minor Low-level disruptions or rule breaks Tardiness, talking out of turn, not following directions
Minor Disrespect to peers or staff Name-calling, minor disagreements
Major Serious or unsafe behavior Fighting, bullying, vandalism
Major Possession of prohibited items Weapons, drugs, tobacco

Schoolwide and Classroom Consequences

Consequences increase in severity based on infraction level, aligned to PBIS principles:

  • Minor Infractions: Verbal warning, loss of privileges, parent contact.
  • Major Infractions: Office referral, behavior contract, in-school suspension, possible out-of-school suspension.

Classroom consequences are consistent with schoolwide guidelines and focus on teaching and reteaching expectations.

  1. Teaching behavioral expectations explicitly (Tier 1)
  2. Using data to guide targeted interventions (Tier 2)
  3. Functional behavior assessment and individualized supports (Tier 3)
  4. Positive reinforcement systems for all students
  5. Staff training and collaborative problem solving
  6. Ongoing progress monitoring and feedback loops

PBIS Forms and Data Collection

Our PBIS program uses a variety of data collection and intervention forms to support behavioral monitoring and decision-making across all three tiers.

Tier 1: Universal Data Collection

  • Tier 1 Behavior Tracking Form: Monitors general student behavior schoolwide and identifies patterns of positive and challenging behaviors.
    Example Tier 1 data resource (Florida PBIS Project)

Tier 2: Targeted Intervention Forms

  • Tier 2 Intervention Progress Form: Used for small group interventions to track student progress and adjust supports accordingly.
    Tier 2 & 3 intervention strategies (NASET)

Tier 3: Intensive Individualized Supports

  • Individual Behavior Plan Data Sheet: Collects detailed data for students with significant behavioral challenges, guiding intensive interventions and functional behavior assessments.
    Tier 3 supports (Florida PBIS Project)

Office Referral and Incident Reporting Forms

  • Office Referral Form: Documents major infractions and disciplinary actions to ensure appropriate follow-up and data-driven decision making.
    Why implement PBIS? (PBIS.org)

All forms are collected regularly and reviewed by the PBIS team to guide interventions, improve behavior supports, and enhance school climate.
